Take Me Home: New York City's Project Reconnect
BY THE PROJECT RECONNECT GROUP
MAY 5, 2015

New York City achieved a new record this year, its homeless population passed 60,000. While escaping poverty in the city remains daunting , some people have a way out of the shelter system. The Department of Homeless Services will buy one-way bus, train, or plane tickets for anyone who can prove they have housing at an out-of-town address.

The program, known as Project Reconnect, has been in place in one form or another since Ed Koch’s tenure as mayor, albeit by different names. But after more than two decades, opinions remain divided about the program. This four part series takes an in-depth look into the program’s history, process, costs and destination trends.
